A beacon of light on vaccine discovery

Pharos Biologicals

Pharos Biologicals, LLC is a privately-owned development stage company that develops vaccines and intellectual property intended to be licensed for commercial development to global pharmaceutical companies that share the company’s values of balancing humanitarian and commercial concerns.

Pharos has set an initial target the objective of developing the first effective vaccine for Zika and intends to develop similar vaccines for other flavi- and influenza viruses.

 

Pharos Biologicals was recently formed with the immediate goal of developing a vaccine for Zika influenza virus, followed by vaccines for other flavivirus and influenza virus pathogens.

Our vaccine design is based on the LAMP (Lysosome-Associated Membrane Protein) vaccine technology that was developed at the Johns Hopkins University School of Medicine (JHUSOM) by Dr. J. Thomas August and patented by JHUSOM. This technology was recently commercially validated by the sale of another company's license of the LAMP patent for allergen vaccines to Astellas, a large global pharmaceutical company, for $300 million. The transaction has been described as the largest patent licence royalty in the history of Johns Hopkins University.

Pharos has the worldwide license to the Johns Hopkins LAMP patents for flaviviruses and influenza, and to the proprietary nanosphere technology that will be used to deliver the vaccine to patients. The company has already initiated an FDA-designed clinical trial for a Zika vaccine, and anticipates that it will be ready to commence Phase 1 human trials by the end of 2016.

Pharos utilizes three cutting edge yet proven technologies that have been developed and patented at Johns Hopkins:

The LAMP Vaccine Platform.

The antigen protein sequences are encoded as an antigen/LAMP chimera construct that offers critical advantages as a vaccine. The DNA vaccine replaces attenuated live virus vaccines and is both much safer and can be more rapidly developed

Nanosphere DNA Vaccine Delivery.

An effective DNA vaccine construct is essential, but it also requires antigen delivery to the immune response cells of the vaccinated subject.  Pharos also holds worldwide licenses to patented nanosphere technologies for delivery of the DNA vaccine construct to the lymphatic system and regional lymph nodes of vaccinated subjects.

Bioinformatic analysis in the design of DNA vaccines.

Developing effective vaccines requires large- scale analysis of pathogen protein structure.  Based on Dr. August’s longstanding work with leading bioinformatics specialists at the National University of Singapore when he was Director of Johns Hopkins Singapore, a bioinformatics subsidiary, Pharos Bioinformatics Pte, Ltd, has been established in Singapore. Pharos Bioinformatics has already analyzed the protein sequences of all reported human Zika viruses for the selection of sequences encoded as antigens in the DNA vaccine.  This facility also supports Pharos' long-terms plans for dengue and other flavivirus pathogens, and influenza.
